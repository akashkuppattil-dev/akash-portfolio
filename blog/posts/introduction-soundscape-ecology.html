Introduction to Soundscape Ecology

February 28, 2023 5 min read Research
soundscape ecology acoustic ecology research methods biodiversity

Soundscape ecology is an emerging field that studies the acoustic environment and its relationship with ecological processes. In this post, I'll introduce the basic concepts and applications of soundscape ecology.

What is a Soundscape?

A soundscape consists of all the sounds present in a particular location, including:

  • Biophony: Sounds produced by organisms (e.g., bird songs, insect stridulation)
  • Geophony: Sounds from non-biological natural sources (e.g., wind, water, thunder)
  • Anthrophony: Sounds produced by human activities (e.g., traffic, construction)

Applications in Ecological Research

Soundscape ecology has numerous applications, including:

  • Biodiversity assessment and monitoring
  • Ecosystem health assessment
  • Studying the impacts of human activities on wildlife
  • Climate change research

Methods and Tools

Common methods in soundscape ecology include:

  • Acoustic indices (e.g., Acoustic Complexity Index, Normalized Difference Soundscape Index)
  • Automated species recognition
  • Spatial and temporal pattern analysis
